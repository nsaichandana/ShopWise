# Problem Statement

## Current Situation

When purchasing products online, users are exposed to thousands of options, technical specifications, reviews, ratings, videos, and opinions.

Although information is widely available, many users still struggle to determine whether a product is actually suitable for their needs.

As a result, users spend significant time researching products and often remain uncertain before making a purchase.

## Problems Faced By Users

### Information Overload

Users are presented with large amounts of information but receive little guidance on what is actually important for their specific situation.

### Lack of Technical Knowledge

Many users do not understand technical specifications such as processor models, RAM requirements, graphics cards, or storage technologies.

### Too Many Choices

Multiple products appear similar, making comparison difficult and increasing decision fatigue.

### Lack of Confidence

Even after researching extensively, users often feel unsure about whether they are making the right decision.

### Fear of Making a Wrong Purchase

Users worry about:

* Overspending
* Buying unnecessary features
* Missing important features
* Purchasing products that do not fit their needs

## Limitations of Existing Platforms

Most e-commerce platforms focus on displaying products, specifications, reviews, and ratings.

The responsibility of evaluating products and matching them to personal requirements is left entirely to the user.

These platforms rarely explain why a specific product is suitable for a particular person.

## Proposed Solution

ShopWise helps users convert their real-world requirements into product recommendations.

The platform gathers information about the user's needs, priorities, and constraints.

It then evaluates products using a rule-based recommendation engine and provides:

* Personalized recommendations
* Match scores
* Clear explanations
* Trade-offs between options

This allows users to make informed decisions with greater confidence and less research effort.
